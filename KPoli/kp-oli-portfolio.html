About

A visionary leader dedicated to Nepal's development and prosperity

Khadga Prasad Sharma Oli is a prominent Nepalese politician and the Chairman of the Communist Party of Nepal (Unified Marxist–Leninist). Born on February 22, 1952, in Terhathum District, Nepal, Oli has dedicated over four decades to public service and political leadership.

He served as the Prime Minister of Nepal multiple times, most notably from 2015 to 2016 and again from 2018 to 2021. His tenure was marked by significant constitutional reforms, economic development initiatives, and strengthening of Nepal's international relations.

Oli is known for his unwavering commitment to communist ideologies and national development. He played a pivotal role in the unification of communist parties in Nepal and was instrumental in the peace process and constitutional development following the 2006 revolution.

Career Timeline

Four decades of political journey and achievements

1970s

Early Political Involvement

Joined the Communist Party of Nepal and became actively involved in student politics and underground activities against the Panchayat system. Played a key role in organizing protests and political movements.

1991

First Election to Parliament

Elected to the House of Representatives for the first time. Served in various ministerial roles including Minister of Home Affairs, where he implemented significant security and administrative reforms.

2015

First Term as Prime Minister

Led the government during the challenging period following the devastating earthquake. Played a crucial role in post-disaster recovery and the historic constitution drafting process that established Nepal as a federal republic.

2018

Second Term as Prime Minister

Focused on economic development, infrastructure projects, and strengthening Nepal's international relations. Implemented policies aimed at poverty reduction and national development.

2021-Present

Party Leadership

Serves as Chairman of CPN (UML), continuing to influence national politics and party strategies. Focuses on party unification and strengthening democratic processes in Nepal.

Major Achievements

Significant contributions to Nepal's development

Constitutional Reforms

Led the historic process of drafting and implementing Nepal's 2015 Constitution, establishing federalism, secularism, and inclusive democracy.

Infrastructure Development

Initiated major infrastructure projects including highways, airports, and energy projects to boost economic growth and connectivity.

International Relations

Strengthened Nepal's foreign relations with neighboring countries and global partners, promoting economic cooperation and diplomacy.

Party Unification

Played a pivotal role in merging communist factions to form the unified CPN (UML), creating a stronger political force in Nepal.
